MSE:
A Multi-Market Framework
for Federal Reserve Policy Analytics

A browser-native implementation of the CME FedWatch methodology extended into a full monetary policy analytics suite: unconditional probability lattice from 30-day ZQ futures, three-way prediction market comparison, SOFR term structure, Nelson-Siegel yield curve fitting, Taylor Rule suite with Judd-Rudebusch ECM, and a Bieri-Chincarini riding-the-yield-curve calculator. No build step. No server. No dependencies.

The EFFR chain converts each month's ZQ futures price into start-of-month and end-of-month rates, isolating the exact point where the Fed changes rates. Non-meeting months serve as anchors that propagate the rate between meeting months.

Anchor rule — non-meeting months
\[\text{EFFR}(\text{End})_{T-1} \;=\; \text{EFFR}(\text{Avg})_{T} \;=\; \text{EFFR}(\text{Start})_{T+1}\]
where \(T\) is any month without an FOMC meeting
Meeting month formula
\[\text{EFFR}(\text{Start}) \;=\; \frac{\text{EFFR}(\text{Avg}) \;-\; \dfrac{M}{N+M} \cdot \text{EFFR}(\text{End})}{\dfrac{N}{N+M}}\]
\(N\) = days from month start through meeting day  ·  \(M\) = remaining days after the meeting
From chain to probabilities
  1. Compute expected EFFR change: EFFR(End) − EFFR(Start)
  2. Divide by 0.25 to get equivalent 25bp moves (e.g., 1.24×)
  3. Split into whole integer (characteristic = 1) and decimal (mantissa = 0.24)
  4. P(base move = 1 × 25bp) = 1 − mantissa = 76%
  5. P(larger move = 2 × 25bp) = mantissa = 24%

The probability tree compounds the per-meeting binary splits into unconditional cumulative probabilities. Each node shows the probability of being at that rate level at that point in time, accounting for all paths to get there.

\[P(\text{node at meeting } M,\; \text{rate } R) = \sum_{R'} P(\text{prior node at } R') \;\times\; P(R' \to R \mid M)\]
Each meeting contributes at most 2 branches.
Reading the lattice
Node opacity
More opaque = higher probability. Nodes below ~0.5% are hidden to reduce visual clutter.
Edge width
Thicker = higher probability weight for that transition branch.

Instantaneous 3M forward
2×r(t+1) − r(t) — marginal rate for each quarter, stripping out the averaging. Leads at inflection points.
\[f_t \;=\; 2\,r_{t+1} - r_t\]

The yield curve tab fits a Nelson-Siegel parametric model to 7 Treasury CMT maturities using 2,000-iteration Nelder-Mead optimization. It then propagates FOMC meeting rate shocks into yield curve scenarios using Bieri-Chincarini (2005) empirical factor loadings.

Nelson-Siegel spot rate
\[y(m) = \beta_0 + \beta_1 \cdot \frac{1-e^{-m/\tau}}{m/\tau} + \beta_2 \cdot \left[\frac{1-e^{-m/\tau}}{m/\tau} - e^{-m/\tau}\right]\]
β₀ = level factor  ·  β₁ = slope factor  ·  β₂ = curvature factor  ·  τ = decay constant
Instantaneous forward rate
\[f(m) = \beta_0 + \beta_1 \,e^{-m/\tau} + \beta_2 \cdot \frac{m}{\tau} \cdot e^{-m/\tau}\]
Scenario shock propagation (Bieri-Chincarini 2005)

Per 100bp FOMC rate change: 2yr +0.50bp, 5yr +0.30bp, 10yr +0.10bp, 30yr +0.05bp. Applied cumulatively at each FOMC meeting to produce the scenario curve fan in the chart.

The Taylor Rule tab implements five distinct policy rule specifications and compares their prescriptions against the CME market-implied path. The Judd-Rudebusch ECM is designated the primary signal source for downstream filter cascades.

Atlanta Fed generalized form (base)
\[\hat{i}_t = \rho \cdot i_{t-1} + (1-\rho)\left[r^* + \pi^* + 1.5(\pi_t - \pi^*) + \beta \cdot \text{gap}_t\right]\]
ρ = smoothing weight  ·  gap = output gap or 2×(u − u*) depending on measure
Judd-Rudebusch ECM (Bieri-Chincarini 2005, Eqs. 23–25) — designated signal
\[i^*_t = r^* + \pi^* + \lambda_1(\pi_t - \pi^*) + \lambda_2 \,\text{gap}_t + \lambda_3 \,\text{gap}_{t-1}\] \[\Delta i_t = \gamma(i^*_t - i_{t-1})\]
Partial adjustment toward the long-run target. Highest R² for FFTR prediction per Bieri-Chincarini Exhibit 7.
Five rule variants
Taylor 1993
α=1.5, β=0.5, GDP output gap. The original specification from Taylor (1993, Carnegie-Rochester).
Balanced / Yellen 2012
α=β=1.0. The "balanced approach" rule highlighted in Yellen's 2012 speech and Fed Tealbook.
Yellen Alt1
Unemployment gap via Okun's Law (gap ≈ −2×(u − u*)). Uses FOMC SEP median longer-run r*.
Clarida-Gali-Gertler
Inertial rule with ρ=0.85 smoothing. Matches the empirical persistence observed in FOMC behavior.
Judd-Rudebusch ECM ★
Dynamic partial adjustment. JR signal feeds the Riding Calculator's filter cascade and the Dashboard regime summary.

The Riding Calculator implements the Bieri-Chincarini (2005) riding-the-yield-curve framework across a 7×5 instrument-horizon matrix. Each cell shows annualized excess HPR and break-even cushion in basis points, with a 5-filter cascade determining the signal quality.

Zero-coupon bond HPR (Bieri-Chincarini 2005, Eq. 12)
\[\text{HPR}_{m,h} = \frac{r_m \cdot m - r_{m-h} \cdot (m-h)}{h} - r_h\]
Annualized excess HPR of an \(m\)-year instrument over horizon \(h\), relative to the \(h\)-period risk-free rate.
Break-even cushion (Eq. 16)
\[\text{Cushion} = r_m - r_h - \Delta r_{\text{break-even}}\]
Yield rise tolerated before the riding strategy underperforms the roll-down. Shown in basis points.
Five-filter suite
① Positive slope
10yr–2yr spread must be positive (normal curve). Active riding requires upward-sloping term structure.
② Positive cushion
Cushion > 0bp. A non-negative break-even removes the downside risk of small rate moves.
③ 75th pctile cushion
Cushion must exceed its historical 75th percentile. Selects only high-conviction rides.
④ Taylor rule signal
JR ECM signal = −1 (cut mode). Riding is most attractive when the Taylor Rule prescribes easing.
⑤ CME expectations
CME 12M path must have negative slope (cuts priced). Market expectations of easing enhance carry.

Ten named ZQ price configurations grouped by historical era. Activating a scenario reprices the entire chain — probability tree, prediction market overlays, yield curve shocks, Taylor prescriptions, riding HPR matrix, and Dashboard — in one click. A second click restores the previous prices. An amber compare overlay applies a second scenario alongside the active one.

How activate / deactivate works
  1. Before applying, current manual prices are saved automatically in state
  2. The scenario's ZQ price overrides are applied to all 12 months
  3. Every tab updates instantly through the shared useFinance signal chain
  4. Click the active scenario chip again to restore your original prices exactly
  5. The compare overlay (amber) applies a second scenario for side-by-side analysis
