The EFFR chain converts each month's ZQ futures price (the average EFFR for that whole month) into start-of-month and end-of-month rates — isolating the exact point where the Fed changes rates.

Anchor rule — non-meeting months
\[\text{EFFR}(\text{End})_{T-1} \;=\; \text{EFFR}(\text{Avg})_{T} \;=\; \text{EFFR}(\text{Start})_{T+1}\]
where \(T\) is any month without an FOMC meeting
Meeting month formula
\[\text{EFFR}(\text{Start}) \;=\; \frac{\text{EFFR}(\text{Avg}) \;-\; \dfrac{M}{N+M} \cdot \text{EFFR}(\text{End})}{\dfrac{N}{N+M}}\]
\(N\) = days from month start through meeting day  ·  \(M\) = remaining days after the meeting
From chain to probabilities
  1. Compute expected EFFR change: EFFR(End) − EFFR(Start)
  2. Divide by 0.25 to get equivalent 25bp moves (e.g., 1.24×)
  3. Split into whole integer (characteristic = 1) and decimal (mantissa = 0.24)
  4. P(base move = 1 × 25bp) = 1 − mantissa = 76%
  5. P(larger move = 2 × 25bp) = mantissa = 24%

The probability tree compounds the per-meeting binary splits into unconditional cumulative probabilities. Each node shows the probability of being at that rate level at that point in time, accounting for all paths to get there.

\[P(\text{node at meeting } M,\; \text{rate } R) = \sum_{R'} P(\text{prior node at } R') \;\times\; P(R' \to R \mid M)\]
Each meeting contributes at most 2 branches.
Reading the lattice
Node opacity
More opaque = higher probability. Nodes below ~0.5% are hidden.
Edge width
Thicker = higher probability weight for that transition.
Solid vs dashed
Solid = base move. Dashed blue = additional 25bp cut (the mantissa branch).

Shows the lower bound, e.g. "3.25%" means the 3.25–3.50% target range.

The Yield Curve tab fits a Nelson-Siegel model to 7 Treasury CMT maturities and propagates per-meeting FOMC scenario shocks along the curve using Bieri-Chincarini empirical factor loadings.

Nelson-Siegel model
\[y(\tau) = \beta_0 + \beta_1 \frac{1 - e^{-\tau/\lambda}}{\tau/\lambda} + \beta_2 \left(\frac{1 - e^{-\tau/\lambda}}{\tau/\lambda} - e^{-\tau/\lambda}\right)\]
\(\beta_0\) = level (long-run)  ·  \(\beta_1\) = slope  ·  \(\beta_2\) = curvature  ·  \(\lambda\) = decay
Factor cards
β₀ — Level
Long-run yield to which the curve converges. Approximately the 30yr CMT.
β₁ — Slope
Short-end loading. Negative when curve is upward-sloping (normal). Equals 3mo minus 30yr spread.
β₂ — Curvature
Hump at intermediate maturities. Positive when medium-term rates deviate from simple interpolation.
λ — Decay / τ
Controls where the curvature hump peaks, typically 2–3 years. Optimized by Nelder-Mead.
Term premium
\(\beta_0 - \text{EFFR}\). Compensation demanded by investors for duration risk beyond the policy rate.
RMSE badge
Fit quality in basis points across the 7 input tenors. Below 5 bp is excellent; above 15 bp signals a regime break.
Scenario shock propagation

Each FOMC meeting applies cumulative rate change to the NS parameters using Bieri-Chincarini (2005) empirical loadings: 2yr +0.50, 5yr +0.30, 10yr +0.10, 30yr +0.05 per 100 bp FFTR shift. The curve fan chart shows the resulting spot curve and instantaneous forward at each meeting date.

Five Taylor Rule variants prescribe where the policy rate "should" be given macroeconomic inputs. The gap between each rule and the CME-implied path is the primary output — it quantifies how hawkish or dovish market pricing is relative to a given reaction function.

The five variants
Taylor 1993
Original rule. α=1.5 on inflation gap, β=0.5 on GDP output gap. Benchmarks all subsequent variants.
α=1.5 · β=0.5
Balanced / Yellen 2012
Equal weight on inflation and employment mandates. Yellen's preferred specification during the dual-mandate era.
α=1.0 · β=1.0
Yellen Alt1
Replaces GDP output gap with unemployment gap via Okun's Law. Uses FOMC SEP r* as neutral rate anchor.
Okun · SEP r*
Clarida-Gali-Gertler (inertial)
Partial-adjustment specification. ρ=0.85 smoothing dampens oscillations and better fits actual Fed behaviour.
ρ=0.85
Judd-Rudebusch ECM ★
Dynamic error-correction specification from Bieri-Chincarini (2005) Eqs. 23–25. Highest R² for FFTR prediction (Exhibit 7). Designated signal source for the Riding filter cascade.
λ₁ · λ₂ · λ₃ · γ

The Riding tab implements the Bieri-Chincarini (2005) riding-the-yield-curve framework. For each instrument–horizon pair, it calculates whether you earn more by buying a longer bond and selling it after a shorter horizon (riding) than by holding an instrument that matures at the horizon date.

Excess holding-period return
\[\text{XHPR}(m, h) = \text{HPR}(m, h) - \text{mmHPR}(h)\]
\(m\) = instrument maturity  ·  \(h\) = holding horizon  ·  mmHPR = money-market alternative (Eq. 8)
\[\text{Cushion}(m,h) = \frac{y(m) - y(h)}{D(m,h)} \times 100\text{ bp}\]
Break-even yield rise the rider can absorb before underperforming the hold-to-maturity alternative (Eqs. 15–16)
The 7 × 5 HPR matrix

Rows: 6-month, 1yr, 2yr, 3yr, 5yr, 10yr, 30yr instruments. Columns: 1mo, 3mo, 6mo, 12mo, 18mo horizons. Each cell shows annualized XHPR (pp) and cushion (bp) with ride/marginal/hold coloring. Click any cell to open the cushion detail panel with a gauge bar showing the cell's percentile relative to the 75th-percentile cutoff.

Five filter rules
①
Positive slope
2yr−3mo spread must be positive. No riding in an inverted curve environment.
②
Positive cushion
Break-even cushion must exceed zero. The most basic riding prerequisite.
③
75th-percentile cushion
Cushion must exceed the 75th-percentile threshold across all cells. High-conviction riding signal only.
④
Taylor rule signal
JR ECM prescribed rate below CME implied — market is pricing too many cuts vs fundamentals, riding safer.
⑤
CME expectations signal
P(≥1 cut) below 50% — futures market not yet pricing easing, making riding a contrarian carry trade.
Duration-neutral barbell

How activate / deactivate works
  1. Before applying, your current manual prices are saved automatically
  2. The scenario's ZQ price overrides are applied to all 12 months
  3. Every tab updates instantly — chain, tree, regime bar, all panels
  4. Click the active scenario again to restore your original prices
