How it works

MSE is a signal chain, not a dashboard. Each of the eleven tabs adds one analytical layer to a single shared state, so every upstream change — a ZQ price adjustment, a macro input, a scenario activation — propagates instantly through the entire suite. The chain runs from raw futures prices to financial stability, answering a single question at each stage: what does this source tell us about the true distribution of future Fed policy that no other source alone can reveal?

The key insight is that futures, prediction markets, the yield curve, policy rules, and financial conditions are not redundant — they represent genuinely different agent types, time horizons, and loss functions pricing the same underlying uncertainty. MSE reads all of them simultaneously and makes their agreement and disagreement explicit.

Purpose: Convert raw ZQ futures prices into a per-meeting EFFR path — the overnight rate the Fed will hold after each decision.

The CME FedWatch methodology uses N/M day-weighting to isolate the pre- and post-meeting EFFR levels within a contract month. Non-meeting months are anchors that propagate the rate between decisions. A fractional move count near 0.5 means maximum uncertainty between two adjacent outcomes; near 0 or 1 means near-certainty of a specific move size.

Key output: Per-meeting base/additional move probabilities → Probability Tree.

Anchor rule — non-meeting months
\[\text{EFFR}(\text{End})_{T-1} \;=\; \text{EFFR}(\text{Avg})_{T} \;=\; \text{EFFR}(\text{Start})_{T+1}\]
where \(T\) is any month without an FOMC meeting
Meeting month formula
\[\text{EFFR}(\text{Start}) \;=\; \frac{\text{EFFR}(\text{Avg}) \;-\; \dfrac{M}{N+M} \cdot \text{EFFR}(\text{End})}{\dfrac{N}{N+M}}\]
\(N\) = days from month start through meeting day  ·  \(M\) = remaining days after the meeting
From chain to probabilities
  1. Compute expected EFFR change: EFFR(End) − EFFR(Start)
  2. Divide by 0.25 to get equivalent 25bp moves (e.g., 1.24×)
  3. Split into whole integer (characteristic = 1) and decimal (mantissa = 0.24)
  4. P(base move = 1 × 25bp) = 1 − mantissa = 76%
  5. P(larger move = 2 × 25bp) = mantissa = 24%

Purpose: Compound per-meeting binary splits into an unconditional lattice showing the full joint distribution of possible rate paths over 12 months.

At each FOMC meeting, prior probability mass splits into hold/cut/hike branches. Nodes accumulate probability multiplicatively. The result is the market's complete probability distribution over where rates end up in 12 months — not just the central scenario.

What to look for: Bright nodes = most likely paths. Horizontal spread at each meeting = policy uncertainty. A single dominant column means high conviction. The amber compare overlay renders a second scenario's distribution in parallel. The final distribution (rightmost) is the key output.

Key output: Terminal rate distribution, P(≥1 cut), expected cuts → anchors all prediction market comparison.

\[P(\text{node at meeting } M,\; \text{rate } R) = \sum_{R'} P(\text{prior node at } R') \;\times\; P(R' \to R \mid M)\]
Each meeting contributes at most 2 branches.

Purpose: Map the transmission of overnight rate expectations into the 3-month term structure, revealing how markets price the Fed's path through the quarterly SOFR forward curve.

The SR3 SOFR strip is independent of the ZQ chain — it prices the secured overnight lending market, not the Fed Funds target. The SOFR–EFFR basis (SR3 minus ZQ quarterly average) measures transmission quality. A basis near zero means clean transmission; widening signals reserve scarcity or credit/liquidity risk premia entering the term structure. The instantaneous 3M forward strips out the quarterly-averaging effect to expose the marginal rate for each quarter.

What to look for: The forward trough marks peak easing intensity — distinct from the terminal rate level. A rising basis as the curve eases is an early warning of transmission breakdown. Basis above 15bp warrants cross-checking against the NFCI and swap line readings in Tab 11.

Key output: SOFR–EFFR basis → PCI transmission quality component (weight: 0.25).

Purpose: Fit the full Treasury term structure to a four-parameter Nelson-Siegel model and propagate FOMC rate shocks into curve scenarios across all maturities.

NS fitting over 7 CMT maturities via 2,000-iteration Nelder-Mead gives four factors: β₀ (level, long-run rate), β₁ (slope, short-end loading), β₂ (curvature, medium-maturity hump), and τ (decay). The term premium — β₀ minus EFFR — is the compensation for duration risk. Negative term premium means the curve has inverted beyond what rate expectations alone justify. Scenario shocks use Bieri-Chincarini (2005) empirical factor loadings applied cumulatively at each FOMC meeting.

What to look for: RMSE above 10bp signals the current curve regime may be poorly captured by the NS form — common at extreme inversions. The scenario fan shows how each meeting's expected move shifts the entire curve. The β₀ decomposition (KVJ safety premium + leverage channel) appears in Tab 10.

Key output: NS factors + scenario shocks → Riding HPR matrix. β₀ decomposition → Balance Sheet Tab 10.

Illustrative curve chart
Nelson-Siegel spot rate
\[y(m) = \beta_0 + \beta_1 \cdot \frac{1-e^{-m/\tau}}{m/\tau} + \beta_2 \cdot \left[\frac{1-e^{-m/\tau}}{m/\tau} - e^{-m/\tau}\right]\]
β₀ = level factor  ·  β₁ = slope factor  ·  β₂ = curvature factor  ·  τ = decay constant
Instantaneous forward rate
\[f(m) = \beta_0 + \beta_1 \,e^{-m/\tau} + \beta_2 \cdot \frac{m}{\tau} \cdot e^{-m/\tau}\]
Scenario shock propagation (Bieri-Chincarini 2005)

Purpose: Benchmark the market-implied rate path against what five systematic policy rules prescribe — revealing whether the market is pricing above or below the rule-consistent rate.

The Taylor−CME gap (prescriptive minus market) is the core signal: positive = market prices looser than the rule warrants; negative = market prices tighter. Five variants cover the range from the original Taylor (1993) specification through the Judd-Rudebusch ECM — the designated signal source for downstream tabs by virtue of its highest R² for FFTR prediction (Bieri-Chincarini 2005, Exhibit 7). A sixth finance-neutral variant (Borio 2016) embeds NFCI into the natural rate estimate.

What to look for: Convergence across rule variants = robust prescription. Divergence = model-sensitive results, treat with caution. The JR ECM gap is the primary downstream signal. A positive JR gap (market below rule) is the necessary condition for the riding signal filter to activate.

Key output: JR ECM signal → Riding filter #4. Finance-neutral gap → Dashboard leaning premium row.

ρ = smoothing weight  ·  gap = output gap or 2×(u − u*) depending on measure
Judd-Rudebusch ECM (Bieri-Chincarini 2005, Eqs. 23–25) — designated signal

Partial adjustment toward the long-run target. Highest R² for FFTR prediction per Bieri-Chincarini Exhibit 7.

Purpose: Apply the Bieri-Chincarini (2005) riding-the-yield-curve framework to identify whether the current curve shape generates positive excess returns for extending duration beyond the money-market horizon.

The 7×5 HPR matrix computes annualized excess holding-period returns and break-even cushions for every combination of instrument maturity (6mo–30yr) and holding horizon (1mo–18mo). Six filters gate the composite riding signal: positive slope, positive cushion, 75th-percentile cushion, Taylor rule signal (JR ECM), CME expectations signal, and SS term-premium put signal (Stein-Sunderam 2018). All six filters passing simultaneously is the strongest possible riding signal.

What to look for: Green HPR cells = favorable riding. The cushion gauge shows how much the curve can flatten before the ride loses money. The barbell panel shows whether optimal duration extension concentrates at a specific maturity or spreads across the curve.

Key output: Composite riding signal → Dashboard stat card.

Zero-coupon bond HPR (Bieri-Chincarini 2005, Eq. 12)
\[\text{HPR}_{m,h} = \frac{r_m \cdot m - r_{m-h} \cdot (m-h)}{h} - r_h\]
Annualized excess HPR of an \(m\)-year instrument over horizon \(h\), relative to the \(h\)-period risk-free rate.
Break-even cushion (Eq. 16)
\[\text{Cushion} = r_m - r_h - \Delta r_{\text{break-even}}\]
Yield rise tolerated before the riding strategy underperforms the roll-down. Shown in basis points.
Five-filter suite
① Positive slope
10yr–2yr spread must be positive (normal curve). Active riding requires upward-sloping term structure.
② Positive cushion
Cushion > 0bp. A non-negative break-even removes the downside risk of small rate moves.
③ 75th pctile cushion
Cushion must exceed its historical 75th percentile. Selects only high-conviction rides.
④ Taylor rule signal
JR ECM signal = −1 (cut mode). Riding is most attractive when the Taylor Rule prescribes easing.
⑤ CME expectations
CME 12M path must have negative slope (cuts priced). Market expectations of easing enhance carry.

The Balance Sheet tab tracks the Fed's two-instrument policy framework: the federal funds rate (EFFR) and the size/composition of the balance sheet. QE expands the balance sheet to ease financial conditions at the zero lower bound; QT shrinks it to normalize. The KVJ (2011) shadow rate decomposes the balance sheet's effect into four channels.

Purpose: Stress-test the entire signal chain against ten historically-calibrated rate path analogs — from the Greenspan soft landing of 1995 to the 2022 re-normalization cycle — enabling rapid exploration of how every downstream tab responds under different Fed policy regimes.

How scenarios work: Activating a scenario reprices all 8 FOMC meeting ZQ contracts simultaneously using the CME N/M weighting formula. The entire chain recalculates instantly — probability lattice, prediction market overlays, NS yield curve scenario fan, Taylor prescriptions, and the riding HPR matrix. A second click restores your previous manual prices exactly. The amber compare overlay holds a second scenario alongside the active one for parallel analysis across all tabs simultaneously.

How to read scenarios: The probability tree under a scenario shows how probability mass concentrates or disperses under that rate path. The Taylor−CME gap shows whether the historical path was rule-consistent or systematically off-prescription. The riding matrix shows which maturities generated positive excess returns in that regime. The compare overlay is most powerful when the active scenario probes a tail risk path while the compare holds the current market consensus.

Scenario calibration: Each scenario is calibrated to a specific historical episode. Easing scenarios range from the single-cut Soft Landing ('95) through the dot-com triple-50bp sequence ('01). Crisis scenarios use GFC ('08) and Volcker disinflation ('82) easing velocities. Tightening scenarios replay the Burns/Miller stagflation re-tightening ('70s) and the 2022 hiking cycle. The historical N/M day-weighting at each meeting is preserved so ZQ prices are internally consistent with the FOMC schedule.

How activate / deactivate works
  1. Before applying, current manual prices are saved automatically in state
  2. The scenario's ZQ price overrides are applied to all 12 months
  3. Every tab updates instantly through the shared useFinance signal chain
  4. Click the active scenario chip again to restore your original prices exactly
  5. The compare overlay (amber) applies a second scenario for side-by-side analysis

Simultaneous changes to ZQ and SR3 that widen the SOFR−EFFR basis are an early warning of transmission risk visible in Tab 5. Getting an API key (standalone use)
  1. Go to console.anthropic.com and sign up
  2. Add a small credit balance ($5 minimum) to activate API access
  3. Generate a key under API Keys — starts with sk-ant-...
  4. Paste into the password field top-right when running the tool standalone
  5. Inside claude.ai the key is injected automatically — leave the field blank
