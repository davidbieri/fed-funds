The EFFR chain converts each month's ZQ futures price (the average EFFR for that whole month) into start-of-month and end-of-month rates — isolating the exact point where the Fed changes rates.

Anchor rule — non-meeting months
\[\text{EFFR}(\text{End})_{T-1} \;=\; \text{EFFR}(\text{Avg})_{T} \;=\; \text{EFFR}(\text{Start})_{T+1}\]
where \(T\) is any month without an FOMC meeting
Meeting month formula
\[\text{EFFR}(\text{Start}) \;=\; \frac{\text{EFFR}(\text{Avg}) \;-\; \dfrac{M}{N+M} \cdot \text{EFFR}(\text{End})}{\dfrac{N}{N+M}}\]
\(N\) = days from month start through meeting day  ·  \(M\) = remaining days after the meeting
From chain to probabilities
  1. Compute expected EFFR change: EFFR(End) − EFFR(Start)
  2. Divide by 0.25 to get equivalent 25bp moves (e.g., 1.24×)
  3. Split into whole integer (characteristic = 1) and decimal (mantissa = 0.24)
  4. P(base move = 1 × 25bp) = 1 − mantissa = 76%
  5. P(larger move = 2 × 25bp) = mantissa = 24%

The probability tree compounds the per-meeting binary splits into unconditional cumulative probabilities. Each node shows the probability of being at that rate level at that point in time, accounting for all paths to get there.

\[P(\text{node at meeting } M,\; \text{rate } R) = \sum_{R'} P(\text{prior node at } R') \;\times\; P(R' \to R \mid M)\]
Each meeting contributes at most 2 branches.
Reading the lattice
Node opacity
More opaque = higher probability. Nodes below ~0.5% are hidden.
Edge width
Thicker = higher probability weight for that transition.
Solid vs dashed
Solid = base move. Dashed blue = additional 25bp cut (the mantissa branch).
Rate label
Shows the lower bound, e.g. "3.25%" means the 3.25–3.50% target range.

The three curves
SR3 implied SOFR
Quarterly-average 3-month SOFR from each SR3 contract. The primary secured lending rate curve.
ZQ quarterly avg EFFR
Average of the three ZQ monthly EFFRs within each quarter. The target rate comparison curve.
Instantaneous 3M forward
2×r(t+1) − r(t) — marginal rate for each quarter, stripping out averaging. Leads at inflection points.
\[f_t \;=\; 2\,r_{t+1} - r_t\]
Forward trough = \(\arg\min_t\; f_t\)  →  quarter where the market prices peak easing intensity

Scenario presets are named ZQ price configurations. Activating one reprices the entire chain — probability tree, regime bar, and all prediction market panels — in one click.

Select a scenario to see its description
How activate / deactivate works
  1. Before applying, your current manual prices are saved automatically
  2. The scenario's ZQ price overrides are applied to all 12 months
  3. Every tab updates instantly — chain, tree, regime bar, all panels
  4. Click the active scenario again to restore your original prices

Four independent refresh buttons fetch live market data. Each targets a different source — a Kalshi outage won't affect your ZQ prices.
